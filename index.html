Step 4: Understanding Your Money (Unit Economics)

üëá

What Does Each Student Actually Make You? üí∞

This is called "unit economics" - basically, how much profit you make from each student after all your costs. This is the most important number in your business!

üßÆ Let's calculate your profit per student (simple version):

  1. Monthly revenue per student: Hours per month √ó hourly rate (e.g., 4 hours √ó ¬£25 = ¬£100)
  2. Direct costs per student: Travel costs + materials + any platform fees (e.g., ¬£8 petrol + ¬£2 worksheets = ¬£10)
  3. Your profit per student: Revenue - Direct costs (e.g., ¬£100 - ¬£10 = ¬£90)
  4. Your hourly profit: Monthly profit √∑ total hours spent (including travel/prep)

‚Ä¢ 4 sessions per month √ó ¬£25 = ¬£100 revenue
‚Ä¢ Costs: ¬£6 travel + ¬£3 materials = ¬£9
‚Ä¢ Net profit: ¬£91 per month
‚Ä¢ Time: 4 hours tutoring + 1 hour travel/prep = 5 hours total
‚Ä¢ Real hourly rate: ¬£91 √∑ 5 = ¬£18.20/hour

Why this matters for scaling:

If you're only making ¬£12/hour after all costs and time, you need to either charge more, reduce costs, or work more efficiently before adding more students. If you're making ¬£25/hour, you have a great foundation for growth!

  • Direct costs for each student (travel, materials, etc.)

  • Real hourly profit rate for each student

Your Business Breakeven Point üìà

Let's figure out exactly how many students you need to cover all your business costs and start making a proper profit.

üéØ Finding your breakeven point:

  1. List your fixed monthly costs: Insurance, phone, website, car costs, etc.
  2. Add your personal target income: How much do you want to earn per month?
  3. Calculate total monthly target: Fixed costs + desired income
  4. Divide by average profit per student: This gives you your target number of students
  5. Check if this is realistic: Can you physically handle this many students?
